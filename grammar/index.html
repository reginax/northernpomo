GRAMMAR

Phonology

There are 10 vowels total because [a, e, i, o, u] are phonetically distinct when lengthened. There are 29 consonants which is categorized by manner of articulation as follows: stops [b, d, p, t, t̪, k, ʔ, ph, th, t̪h, kh, p’, t’, t̪’, k’], affricates [c, c’, ch, ch’, ts, ts’], fricatives [s, sh, h], nasals [m, n], laterals [l], and glides [w, y]. As seen with the large number of stops and affricates, the phonetic inventory of Northern Pomo contains a large number of obstruents. Though not a tonal language, Northern Pomo has varying pitches based on syllable structure, which is CV(C) with no word- and syllable-initial consonant clusters. Some examples of varying pitches are that long vowels have low pitch, and verbs in certain imperatives and interrogatives end with a falling pitch on the last syllable (O’Connor 1987:9). The basic word classes are verbs, nouns, adjectives, and prepositions.

Morphology

Northern Pomo is agglutinative as seen with phrases where morphemes retain their original meaning even when joined; for instance "let’s sleep" is simamiti-ya or "sleep-let’s". Additionally, most derivational or inflectional elements are suffixes, with an example being ta- which denotes multiplicity or repetition of an action (O’Connor 1987:55).

Syntax

In regards to syntax, Northern Pomo is a verb-final language so arguments of the main clause precede verb phrases (VP). Other rules include NP -> (QP) N (AP*) DET and PP -> NP PP (O’Connor 1987:10-12). Additionally, most derivational or “sleep-let’s” (Ko et al. 2014). Verbs in the language are inflected in tense, aspect and mood; specifically there are 3 tense and aspect categories: present or past imperfect, past, and progressive. Different suffixes indicate these combinations of tense, aspect, and mood, such as -khe in “purpose clauses” for future tense and mode (O’Connor 1987:43-45).
